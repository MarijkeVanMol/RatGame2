You have arrived at the second part of my process. In this part, I realized that I was looking in the wrong direction. I wanted to find ‘inner peace’, but was looking at external stimuli.

I researched breathing techniques (like deep breathing) and read ‘Frazzled’ by Ruby Wax. Mindfulness is a tool which allows one’s mind to be more conscious and grounded, which in turn helps a stressed mind to recognize the signs before it goes all godzilla.

The connection between the mind and body is very important, for they influence each other. Breathing is one way to connect both. If you are stressed you might forget to inhale or do it very quickly. Breathe slowly and the body relaxes. And guess what’s key in mindfulness? Breathing.

Also, not letting your thoughts stray. Meditation is a practice is staying calm and present. Especially being present, is something the teaclub in my head doesn’t like very much.
